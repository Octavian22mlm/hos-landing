Rules for Success
80% of the call is your state. Read it once — then you’ve got it.
1
Your state is 80% of the call
30 to 60 seconds of real movement before the call — dance, jump, smile wide in the mirror. You come from abundance, not from the need to convince. A warm voice comes from your state; you can’t fake it.
2
Read the script out loud, at least 3 times
The first time you read it, the second you get used to it, the third the hesitations disappear. Your vocal cords memorize the pattern, not just your mind. A script read silently = a salesman’s tone the client can hear on the call.
3
Say the words EXACTLY as written
“It’s wearing you down,” not “it worries you.” Every word is chosen to get around a resistance. Make HOW you say it natural (rhythm, “look,” the name), but keep WHAT you say (the questions, the mirroring, the closing lines).
4
What to do when the client asks you awkward questions during the script
There are 2 different situations:
Situation 1 — premature question
(at the start of the conversation, BEFORE presenting the product)
If the client asks you:
• “Is this MLM by any chance?”
• “What’s it about?”
• “What are you trying to sell me?”
• “Where did you hear about this?”
Your reply (always smile 😊) — validate the question + promise a guaranteed answer + defer WHEN you answer:
“I love your questions and none of them will go unanswered 😊 — we’ll get there.”
→ Then GO BACK to the next question in the script.
⚠️ Principle: the script works ONLY if you follow the structure. Trust the system 😊
Situation 2 — question after the brief pitch
(after you’ve mentioned the product)
If the client asks:
• “How exactly does it work?”
• “What ingredients does it contain?”
• “How much does it cost?”
• “How is it delivered?”
• “What’s the difference compared to [competitor]?”
⚠️ The danger: if you answer now, you’re doing the presentation on the phone → you lose the reason for the meeting → the client no longer shows up.
Your reply (always smile 😊) — validate + gladly defer to the meeting + redirect toward the date:
“I love that you have good questions that add value to the conversation. I’ll gladly answer them when we meet — that’s exactly the point [of the meeting]. I wouldn’t want any question left unanswered.”
→ Then REDIRECT toward setting the date.
The meta-rule for both situations
• Never a direct “NO”
• Never a blunt refusal
• Always with a smile 😊
• Validate the question FIRST (“I love that...”)
• Promise a guaranteed answer
• You decide WHEN you answer
5
The silence after the question = your strength
If it gets awkward, hold on. They’ll speak first. Don’t fill the silence.
6
If you freeze, you have a safety net
“Let me phrase what I wanted to ask better...” — and you’re back to the script. Nothing is lost.
7
The presentation length: say it exactly, calmly
Without downplaying it, without inflating it. Exactly the value you chose in the module.

How to fill it in for a good result
The quality of your answers = the quality of the script.
Here are the fields that make the difference. Compare wrong (left) with right (right):
Tell us about your client
✕ NOT LIKE THIS
“I’ve known her for a while, she’s ok”
✓ LIKE THIS
“She’s always exhausted after her shifts, sleeps badly, has no energy for anything”
Why: the concrete situation is the anchor on which the bridge to the product is built. Vague → generic script.
Qualities you value in this person
✕ NOT LIKE THIS
“nice, all right”
✓ LIKE THIS
“Empathetic, organized, reliable”
Why: specifics → a reasoned compliment, not empty praise that sounds fake.
Your last interaction with her / him
✕ NOT LIKE THIS
empty / “I don’t remember”
✓ LIKE THIS
“last week, over coffee — she told me she’s wiped out from exhaustion”
Why: a real hook for the opening + mirroring on her exact words.
Professional status
✕ NOT LIKE THIS
“she works” / “she has a job”
✓ LIKE THIS
“Nurse, long night shifts”
Why: it calibrates the profile (analytical? experienced?) and gives a concrete basis for the compliment.
Your concrete experience with the product
✕ NOT LIKE THIS
“it’s great, I really liked it”
✓ LIKE THIS
“I was exhausted after work; after ~3 weeks I sleep better and have energy all day”
Why: real experience = the authentic bridge. Vague → it sounds just like an ad.
The golden rule: specific + real beats generic + vague. And if you don’t know something — leave it blank. Honestly-blank beats made-up.
